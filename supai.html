Supai

Located about four hours east of Las Vegas Nevada you will find the small Indian village of Supai. Nestled deep within the harsh Arizona desert and surrounded by thousand foot canyons this oasis features the most wondrous blue green water you'll ever see. The unique water originates from a natural spring which runs its course through winding canyons until it reaches its confluence with the mighty Colorado River all while taking a picturesque plunge over six magnificent waterfalls. The journey into this paradise is not easy however; you must first acquire a special permit issued by the Supai tribe. 1,200 feet of winding switchbacks and 10 miles of desert later you'll reach the campground. You must pack in all your gear and food and you must take it all with you when you leave. Once you're settled in at your camp spot of choice you can enjoy the sites. There are six waterfalls to see spread out along eight miles of river. Your hiking is not complete even when you get to camp! Permits sell out for the entire year in about 10 minutes so be online February 1st with some backup dates because you'll be lucky to get anything let alone the dates you want.
