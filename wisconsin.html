Wisconsin

Something about gliding across the water under the sky is tremendously relaxing despite paddling non-stop for hours! If you're up for camping on some small islands that according to the National Parks Service has the highest concentration of black bears in the world, the Apostle Islands are for you. Just a few miles off the coast of Bayfield Wisconsin on Lake Superior lies an archipelago of 20 islands where you can really get away. We definitely don't recommend doing this on your own as weather conditions on the lake are unpredictable producing 2-4 foot swells almost instantly. Check out Living Adventure Tours for your trip. They provide everything and their guides really know what they're doing. You literally just have to show up! You can bring some of your own gear to cut some cost. They will take you through a basic safety course teaching you how to escape your kayak if you roll upside down as well as how to get back in on the water. Although we did not flip on our trip we came pretty darn close when hit with some 4ft waves in our 21ft tandem.

Each day we kayaked for 6-8 hours covering about 10-12 miles with some stops along the way. Each island is unique with amazing views and soft sandy beaches. The real appeal of the Apostle Islands are the caves that line the cliffs. You can kayak through some of them and at some points you'll be pressed down face first into the hull of your kayak by the narrow slots just big enough to fit through. When it's time to setup camp, the guide prepared the food while we relaxed on the beach. When we went in late summer the bugs were so bad we had to buy head nets and even then the black flies could bite you through your long sleeved shirt.
