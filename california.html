California

There's way too much to see in California to describe it all on here so we'll focus on a few cheap adventures. First take the drive through the Redwood National Park. Literally drive through a Redwood tree at Chandelier Tree. There is really no way to describe the magnitude of these trees and the humbling perspective you'll feel standing next to one. You could spend an entire trip camping and exploring the forest here but onward we go.

Drive on down highway 1 to the small coastal town of Sea Ranch about 100 miles north of San Francisco. This tiny town of 1,300 people boasts some spectacular ocean views, bluffs and black sand beaches. Time the tied right and spend a few hours exploring tide pools and watching seals pop in and out of the water. If you're really lucky and it's the right season you might see some whales from shore.

Head on into San Francisco and cross over the Golden Gate Bridge on your way to Alcatraz! Alcatraz Cruises will get you to the island for a nominal price. These trips fill fast so plan ahead and get a ticket early, I mean a day or so before you plan on going. Once you're on the island you can explore for as long as you like before catching a boat back. You'll get a headset with a self-paced audio tour that describes the history and features of the prison. This is pretty neat as you're not at the mercy of a guide or someone else's schedule. Tip: Get wet! If it's a particularly bad day and the bay is choppy, stand at the very front of the boat while everyone else is huddled inside!
