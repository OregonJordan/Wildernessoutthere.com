Oregon

Oregon is known world-wide for many different things but for us it's the forest, mountains and beaches oh and the Tillamook Cheese Factory! But of all the places and things to see here the absolute must is the Columbia River Gorge. The cliffs lining the mighty Columbia River will have you feeling like you're in Alaska. The wind gusts will take you to the black sand beaches of Iceland and back. There are so many waterfalls to see here you can walk a mile and see three or four. The tallest and most popular waterfall in Oregon State is Multnomah Falls at 620 ft. I would agree that this is a beautiful waterfall but I would say Tunnel Falls is even better. Also located in the Gorge, Tunnel Falls is not easy to get to and requires a 12 mile round trip hike to see. But when you get there it's all worth it. If you can stomach the narrow path suspended 100 feet above the base, you'll be rewarded with a tunnel behind the falls. Eight years adventuring the Gorge and I'm just now feeling like I've seen most of it. But the journey never ends.

Head to the mountains where at 5,000ft the summit of Tom Dick and Harry Mountain gives an up close and personal view of Mount Hood. Trek a little further up Mount Hood to see the frozen waters of Tamanawas Falls form 20 foot icicles. Strap on your snowshoes, pull up them Gaiters and hoof along the shores of the White River through waist deep powder. Walk across the frozen surface of Twin Lakes through fresh snowfall for a truly peaceful experience.

Not up for all that? That's quite alright. Head south for an easier hike through Silver Falls State Park. The 9 mile loop hike is long but easy to walk and provides up close views of 10 waterfalls some of which allow you to walk behind. There is a park fee here but this loop hike is one of the best.

 Tips

  • Adventure during fall or winter
  • Don't pay for tours, most things are free
  • Pack along some rain gear
  • Avoid the crowds of spring and summer
  • Know where you are going before hand
  • Stick to your plan and stay on trail
  • Bring cash for parking
  • Start early, most places get busy
  • Know your own abilities when
    choosing your adventure
  • Account for traffic at all times
  • A wetsuit will get you places
    you never imagined
