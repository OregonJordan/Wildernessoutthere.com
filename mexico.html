Mexico

We didn't spend a lot of time in Cozumel but we definitely saw it all. Well on land that is. Cozumel is popular for its scuba diving which we do not do...yet! So what did we do here? We rented a jeep and drove everywhere. Navigating this little island is pretty easy if you glance at a map you'll be fine. I would warn you about the traffic laws however, there are none. Besides driving with some basic sense anything goes at least that is how it felt. With that in mind hit the road and check out the many scattered Mayan ruins. Don't waste your time hiring a guide instead do it for free at your own pace with a nominal entrance fee at a few of the ruins. The mainland side (west) of the island is where all the resorts are located and in stark contrast to that of the east side of the island where it feels almost deserted. Which deserted is what we like! The northern part of the island is pretty much off limits and if for some reason you're able to get up there you'll probably run into military check points which in this case we opted to avoid. But alas there is plenty to see elsewhere. San Gervasio for example is one of the largest Mayan complexes on the island. Here you can spend some time strolling through the tropical forest exploring the scattered ruins. Iguanas abound here and have no care about resting in the sun along the path. At the southern point of the island is Punta Sur Park where you can see crocodiles. In the park are some smaller ruins and a pretty neat light house called Faro Celarain where you can climb to the top for a fantastic view.

Some tips about visiting Cozumel. Cash is king and it is custom to tip for everything. Some Spanish language skills would be handy but is not necessary for the most part. If you rent a vehicle make sure you buy the full coverage, you'll thank me later. Be vigilant when driving down some of the single lane dirt roads, it's inevitable you'll round a corner with a large truck barreling down on you. Getting gas requires some basic math skills, a lot of assertiveness with a touch of poker face. Bring bug spray otherwise, "if you stop walking they eat you alive"!
