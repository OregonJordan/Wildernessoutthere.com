Iceland

Planning a trip to Iceland takes some work. I would recommend spending at least six days there because most things you're going to want to see are spread out requiring a lot of driving. Flights to Iceland are super cheap considering the distance however; once you're there you'll be dropping quite a bit of cash on food, gas and pretty much everything else. The currency in Iceland is the Krona which right now is 100 to 1 dollar. Sounds great right, well not exactly. The cheapest meal we could find was Taco Bell which cost around $15 for two tacos and a drink! If you rent a car, don't! Rent a four-wheel drive SUV because many of the roads require 4wheel drive, literally there are signs saying you can't go on the road otherwise. Your car tires will not make it and you definitely don't want to get stranded anywhere. There is nothing around and getting help will take hours. Be prepared for the cost of gas. The gas stations outside of town are self-serve and are easy enough but you'll be spending about $100 a day in gas plus you'll need to buy a gas card first thing.

Buy a map of Iceland and plan your route carefully to maximize your experience. Navigating Iceland is pretty easy as there really is only one road that goes around the whole country. Be aware that most of that road travels over endless fields of glacier run off and in our case, was washed away entirely! Which brings me to my next tip, have a backup plan just in case... the road is gone! All in all Iceland is a multi-trip experience. There are the tourist traps which are ok but the real adventure is away from the crowd and if you're willing to do your own planning, you can see everything a tour guide will charge you for, for free.

Our favorite and probably the most spectacular thing we did during this trip was Reykjadalur Hot Springs. This geothermal river nestled up in the mountains above the small Icelandic town of Hveragerdi provides panoramic views of the Northern Lights! It's totally free and the whole river is a hot spring which gets hotter as you climb higher up the mountain. The temperature ranges from around 70℉ to over 100℉.
