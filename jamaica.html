Jamaica

Oh Jamaica, what can I say. I think this trip was the one that really tested my metal. Let me just say that now when we travel we have some pretty good travel insurance! No one needed to use it thankfully but there were some times where inches made the difference. What makes me say all this is the same thing that is so amazing about Jamaica, they don't coddle you there. There are no safety rails or harnesses to tuck you in or signs to tell you how deep the water is. We wanted to get away from the tourists so we headed to The Blue Hole up in the forested mountains of Ocho Rios. Known as the "Secret Falls" it is undoubtedly one of Jamaica's hidden treasures featuring tropical waterfalls and swimming holes. You can cliff jump, rope swing and BASE jump from the middle of a waterfall. That's right, from the middle of a waterfall which means you have to walk down the surface of the waterfall and jump off. One of the locals dove head first into a 3x3 foot hole in the rocks only to surface meters away from another tiny hole! If you're feeling up for a water cave you can swim under the falls and climb out the side through a narrow passage. We highly recommend this place although as I'm writing this it looks like social media has made The Blue Hole a tourist trap so take it as it is I guess.

You're in Jamaica! Try some authentic Jamaican Jerk. We recommend Scotchies for a taste of the real deal. They roast the meat over a wood fired grill which they cover with sheets of metal roofing to hold in the heat. Thankfully they serve the sauce on the side which for us, a dime sized taste was plenty. I would tell you the experience was worth it only after I could feel my face again! Tipping is expected and although Jamaica has a rough feel and plenty of critics the people we met along the way were nice enough. Time is one thing you will have to get used to very quickly here. Jamaican time is not on any clock!
